Fire in Focus: An Analytical Approach to Analyzing Wildfires

Project Summary

Wildfires are increasingly becoming a global challenge, especially in regions like California, where they threaten both human life and the environment. This project leverages machine learning and deep learning techniques to develop a model to predict wildfire occurrences, integrating both satellite data and meteorological conditions such as temperature, humidity, wind speed, and precipitation. Using data from the NASA FIRMS API and Open-Meteo, we analyzed wildfire occurrences in Southern California from 2020 to 2024. The project includes geospatial and temporal analyses to uncover patterns in wildfire frequency and density. It also explores how weather factors influence the likelihood of wildfires. The dataset contains fire and non-fire occurrences and will be used to train machine learning models to predict wildfires, providing actionable insights for effective wildfire management. We found that temperature and humidity are the most significant factors in predicting wildfire outbreaks, with most occurrences happening during the hottest months, June to September. This model could help in implementing early-warning systems and better resource allocation for wildfire prevention and control efforts in areas of high risk.
